What is Aperture?

Aperture is the opening in a lens that controls how much light reaches your camera sensor. It also shapes depth of field â€” the zone of accepted sharpness in your image.

Learn how stopping down (higher f/numbers) and opening up (lower f/numbers) affect exposure and background blur. Try the simulator below.

Quick facts

Aperture size

Lower f-numbers (e.g. f/1.8) = wider opening = more light + shallower depth of field.

Depth of field

Smaller aperture (higher f-number like f/16) increases depth of field â€” more of the scene is in focus.

Exposure

Aperture is one of the three pillars of exposure. Changing aperture requires compensation with shutter speed or ISO.
